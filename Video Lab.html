Look at these Birds

I used to have this huge tree in my backyard.
I did not like that tree because it would stink up the place when it grew berries.
The tree was big, so big it broke under its own weight, nice.
What wasn't nice was that all the birds in my area had stopped coming to my yard.
I dedicate this page to some of the birds I rarely see now because that tree is no longer here.

This is a Mourning Dove


This species of birds are interesting. They are related to street pigeons, I call those sky rats.
Mourning doves however are more interesting in my opinion because they have a very distint coo.
You can hear it in the video.

This here is a Northern Red Cardinal


As you can see they are very red. Male cardinals are vibrant red while the females tends to be more brown.
The best part about these birds is that they can make a pew pew noise, much like a cheap laser gun.
You can hear the laser gun part at around 0:14 seconds of the video.

The Monk Parakeet


Most people are pleasently surprised when they see one of these perched around a tree or a powerline.
They are even more surprised when they realize monk parakeets tend to come in large groups of 20 or more.
A group of them together are called a company or a pandemonium. You can guess why.
Not much to them besides the fact they are parakeets so they can learn phrases and are bright green.

These are only a small portion of some of the birds I see in my yard and the fact they are no longer around makes it kind of lonely.
Well, at least that tree is no longer here to stink up the place.
